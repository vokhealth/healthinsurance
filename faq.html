Frequently Asked Questions

Medi-Share is a healthcare sharing ministry where members share medical expenses among each other. It's not traditional insurance but can be a cost-effective alternative for some.

HMO plans require you to use a specific network of doctors and need referrals to see specialists. PPO plans offer more flexibility to see out-of-network doctors without referrals but usually cost more.

Open Enrollment for health insurance usually happens once a year, typically between November and December. It's the time when you can sign up for or change your health coverage for the upcoming year.

A Special Enrollment Period allows you to enroll in or change your health insurance outside the Open Enrollment window if you experience qualifying life events like marriage, birth of a child, or loss of other coverage.

Tax credits depend on your household income and size. We help screen for eligibility during your free consultation to ensure you get the best value.

We work with plans that have strong networks, including the United PPO network, which has millions of doctors nationwide, so you can often keep your preferred providers.
