Frequently Asked Questions

Medi-Share is a healthcare sharing ministry where members share medical costs among each other. It’s not traditional insurance but can be a cost-effective alternative for some.

HMO plans require you to use doctors within a network and get referrals for specialists, which often means lower costs. PPO plans offer more flexibility to see out-of-network providers but usually at higher costs.

Open Enrollment is the yearly period when you can sign up for or change your health insurance plan without needing a qualifying event. It usually runs from November 1 to December 15.

A Special Enrollment Period is a limited time outside Open Enrollment when you can sign up or change plans due to qualifying life events like marriage, birth of a child, or loss of other coverage.

Subsidy eligibility depends on your household income and size. We can help screen and guide you on whether you qualify for financial assistance to lower your premiums.

We work with plans that include the United PPO network, giving you access to millions of doctors nationwide, so chances are good your doctors are covered.
